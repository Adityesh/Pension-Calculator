About Pension Calculator


There are vast differences in pension rules that are applicable in different states. Central Government has it own Pension Rules for its employees. The number of central government employees is about 31 Lakhs. They are entitled to different Pensionary benefits like pension, gratuity, leave salary, CGEGIS etc. Different types of emoluments are given in case of superannuation, voluntary retirement(VR) and death.The entire process of acknowdging differences in pension calculation is cumbersome and not consolidated. With our simplistic tool anyone can derive his/her pensionary benefits.


Our Mission

Presently, the retiree or the spouse of deceased employees has to apply for pension, gratuity etc on the newly designed "Bhavisya" portal. While applying they find it difficult to calculate the following.

  1. Monthly pension after commutation
  2. Commutation value
  3. Total quality service
  4. Amount of gratuity
  5. Amount of leave salary
  6. CGEGIS amount

This calculator eliminates the need of manual computation and processing of the input data. With this calculator the user can obtain the exact values of the benefits by providing required input data.

In this age of technology, nothing should become a barrier for seeking information on pensionary benefits. Further, one who plans for voluntary retirement should know the supra before applying for VR. The lower rank employees and spouse of deceased working personnels are generally oblivious and ignorant to the entire process of pension calculation and remain in consistent dilemma about their pensionary benefits.

Choose the type of your retirement

Superannuation

Superannuation

A superannuation pension shall be granted to a Government servant who is retired on his attaining the age of superannuation. As of now the superannation age for central government employee is 60 years from his/her date of birth.

VRS

Voluntary Retirtement Scheme

Any Government servant can apply for voluntary retirement, three months in advance, only after the completion of twenty years of his qualifying service, provided there is no vigilance or Departmental Enquiry pending/initiated against him/her.

DEATH PENSION

Death Pension

The Death Pension, which may also be referred to as Survivors Pension benefit, is a tax-free monetary benefit payable to a low-income, un-remarried surviving spouse and/or unmarried child/child(ren) of a deceased Veteran with wartime service.
